# Write a LinkedIn post recapping the last 24 hours of AI news

Write a single LinkedIn post recapping the most significant developments in the AI world from the last 24 hours.

IMPORTANT — NO HARDCODED DATE: Do not write any fixed calendar date into the post or the process. At runtime, first determine the current date yourself (check the system date / a reliable source), then search the web for AI news published within the trailing 24 hours from that moment. The post itself should read naturally without stamping a specific date (e.g. use framing like 'in the last 24 hours' or 'today' rather than an explicit date), unless a specific event's own date is essential context.

SCOPE:
- Research: Run fresh web searches to identify the top 4–6 AI stories from the last 24 hours. Prioritize high-signal, verifiable developments (major model/product launches, big-tech and national AI announcements, notable hires, funding, regulation/legal, safety reports). Verify each story against a credible source before including it.
- Only include stories that genuinely fall within the last 24 hours at runtime. Do not reuse example stories from prior drafts — re-research live.

DELIVERABLE:
- One ready-to-publish LinkedIn post.
- Tone: professional but punchy, first-person, thought-leadership style suitable for a founder/exec audience.
- Length: ~180–220 words.
- Structure: a strong hook opening line, 3–5 concise highlights (short punchy lines or a tight bulleted/emoji list are fine for LinkedIn), and a closing takeaway or question to drive engagement.
- Add a small set of relevant hashtags at the end (roughly 3–6, e.g. #AI #ArtificialIntelligence plus topic-specific ones).
- Keep claims accurate and grounded in the sourced stories; do not fabricate figures, names, or events. Where a specific number or name is used, it must come from a verified source.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
- Provide the final post text ready to copy-paste.
- Below the post, include a short list of the source links used for each story (for the author's reference; not part of the post body).

ASSUMPTIONS (adjust if desired): professional-but-punchy first-person tone, ~180–220 words, 3–6 hashtags, engagement-oriented closing line.
